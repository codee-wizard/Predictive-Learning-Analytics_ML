# Performance Intervention

## Using Prediction Responsibly

Predicted scores and learner categories are support signals, not final labels.
They should guide intervention and reflection, not discourage students. The
coach should explain predictions in plain language and focus on controllable
actions.

## Predicted Score

A predicted score estimates likely performance from available academic and
behavioural features. It should be presented with humility because predictions
can change when student habits, preparation, or understanding improve.

## Pass Or Fail Status

Pass/fail classification is useful for early warning. If a student is predicted
to fail, the coach should recommend immediate support, fundamentals review,
short daily sessions, and practice tests. The tone should remain encouraging.

## Learner Category

Learner category helps tune the pace of coaching. At-risk students need simpler
steps and more scaffolding. Average students need structured practice and
feedback. High-performing students need challenge and deeper transfer tasks.

## Math Score Intervention

Low math scores usually require checking arithmetic fluency, algebra basics,
word problem translation, formula use, and error patterns. The student should
start with prerequisite concepts before advanced problems.

## Reading Score Intervention

Low reading scores usually require main idea practice, inference practice,
vocabulary in context, evidence-based answer selection, and summarising. The
student should practise explaining why wrong options are wrong.

## Study Hours

More hours do not automatically produce better results. Study quality matters.
If study hours are low, increase consistency gradually. If hours are already
high but performance is weak, improve strategy: active recall, targeted
practice, and error review.

## Test Preparation

Structured test preparation can improve performance because it combines content
review, timed practice, exam format familiarity, and feedback. Students who have
not completed test preparation should start with a diagnostic quiz and a weekly
revision plan.

## Parent Education And Background Features

Background features can help model predictions but should not be treated as
destiny. The coach should avoid making personal judgments from demographic
features. Advice should focus on study behaviours, practice, feedback, and
available support.

## At-Risk Action Plan

An at-risk action plan should prioritise fundamentals, daily short practice,
mistake logs, and teacher or peer support. The first week should focus on one or
two high-impact topics rather than trying to fix everything at once.

## Average Action Plan

An average learner action plan should include mixed practice, weekly quizzes,
topic rotation, and moderate challenge problems. The goal is to convert partial
understanding into reliable exam performance.

## High-Performer Action Plan

A high-performer action plan should maintain accuracy while increasing
difficulty. Recommended tasks include challenge sets, explaining solutions,
timed practice, and connecting multiple concepts in one problem.
