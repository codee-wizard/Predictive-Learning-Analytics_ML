# Writing Skills

## Clear Writing

Clear writing has a purpose, a logical structure, precise language, and evidence
that supports the main point. Good writing is not only grammatically correct; it
also guides the reader through the writer's thinking.

## Paragraph Structure

A strong academic paragraph usually contains a topic sentence, evidence,
explanation, and a link back to the main idea. The PEEL method is useful:

Point: state the idea.

Evidence: provide proof or example.

Explanation: explain how the evidence supports the idea.

Link: connect back to the question or next point.

## Thesis Statement

A thesis statement presents the main argument of an essay. It should be
specific, arguable, and focused. A weak thesis is too broad or only states a
fact. A strong thesis shows the writer's position and direction.

## Essay Structure

A standard academic essay has an introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion.
The introduction gives context and thesis. Body paragraphs each develop one main
idea. The conclusion synthesises the argument without simply repeating every
sentence.

## Evidence

Evidence can include facts, examples, data, quotations, or references to a text.
Evidence must be explained. Dropping a quotation without analysis does not show
understanding.

## Coherence

Coherence means ideas flow logically. Use transitions to show addition,
contrast, cause, result, example, and conclusion. However, transitions cannot
fix disorganised thinking; the order of ideas must also make sense.

## Sentence Variety

Writing improves when sentence lengths and structures vary. Too many short
sentences can sound choppy. Too many long sentences can confuse readers. A good
revision strategy is to read aloud and notice where the sentence rhythm feels
awkward.

## Grammar Priorities

High-impact grammar areas include subject-verb agreement, tense consistency,
pronoun reference, punctuation, sentence fragments, run-on sentences, and
parallel structure.

## Revision

Revision is more than correcting spelling. A useful revision order is: check
argument, check organisation, improve evidence, improve clarity, then proofread
grammar and punctuation.

## Common Writing Mistakes

Common mistakes include vague thesis statements, unsupported claims, repetitive
wording, weak topic sentences, unclear pronouns, overlong sentences, and
conclusions that introduce new arguments.

## Academic Integrity

Students should write in their own words, cite borrowed ideas where required,
and avoid submitting AI-generated or copied text as original work. AI can be
used ethically for brainstorming, feedback, practice questions, and revision
guidance when allowed by the institution.
